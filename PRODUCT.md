# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Guests / Travelers:** People researching and booking luxury Himalayan retreats and boutique consulting stays. Browsing aspirationally — on mobile as often as desktop — already imagining themselves at the property before they book. Expect the portal to feel as curated as the experience itself.

**Property Owners:** Business owners and property managers tracking listings, bookings, guest communications, and revenue. Using primarily on desktop to run their business. Expect professional efficiency without losing the premium context they've signed onto.

**Platform Admins:** Internal operators managing the full ecosystem — users, properties, bookings, inquiries, campaigns, and audit trails. Data-heavy, efficiency-critical, task-switching constantly.

## Product Purpose

Salt Route Consulting connects discerning travelers to curated luxury retreats and consulting experiences in the Himalayas and beyond. The platform handles discovery, booking, and relationship management across three personas — guests who stay, owners who host, and admins who operate. Success means a guest feels the quality of the experience before they arrive, an owner manages their business without friction, and an admin maintains full visibility without drowning in complexity.

## Brand Personality

Warm, sharp, unhurried. The confidence of knowing you offer something rare, without announcing it loudly. Like arriving at a well-run boutique hotel: everything is where you expect it, nothing is overdone, and the quality is obvious without a sign saying so.

References: Airbnb Host Dashboard (property-native warmth, hospitality vocabulary), Notion / Craft Docs (generous whitespace, inviting to live in, hierarchy through weight not decoration).

## Anti-references

- **Booking.com / OTA platforms:** Bright orange urgency, cluttered deal-hunting, price comparison anxiety. Nothing luxury about it.
- **SAP / Salesforce / enterprise dashboards:** Dense tables, corporate navy, modal hell, no breathing room, zero personality.
- **Generic SaaS gray soup:** Gray-on-gray, blue CTA buttons, could be any product in any category. Forgettable on arrival.
- **Trendy glassmorphism:** Frosted cards, purple/pink gradients, blur-everything aesthetics. Overdesigned and dated.

## Design Principles

1. **Hospitality first.** Every interface is a warm welcome, not a form to fill. Language, spacing, and hierarchy should feel like a knowledgeable host, not a system.
2. **Confident restraint.** Bold typography and generous space carry the luxury signal. Decoration is never the answer — let the content breathe and the brand speak through proportion.
3. **One design language, three registers.** Admin, owner, and guest portals share the same visual vocabulary (tokens, type scale, spacing rhythm). Differentiate by tone and density, not by inventing separate systems.
4. **Mobile is first class.** Guests book on phones. Owners check bookings on the go. The experience cannot degrade below a certain threshold on small screens.
5. **Navigation earns trust.** Users should always know where they are, where they can go, and what the most important action is on any screen. No guessing, no hunting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design-led. Fix obvious contrast and keyboard issues throughout, but no formal WCAG target is required for this project. Avoid motion-heavy interactions that could be disorienting. Color choices should not rely solely on hue to convey meaning.
